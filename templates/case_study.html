The Challenge

When the Knight Foundation began redesigning their website (KnightFoundation.org), they were on multiple content management systems they’d outgrown, and the site couldn’t handle traffic spikes. Knight needed a future-proof solution that could accommodate myriad pieces of content and content types (blogs, grant data, publications, grantee profiles)—but also layouts, interactions, and more.

Why Django?

The versatility of Django’s applications meant Knight wouldn’t have to re-platform years later, as unanticipated content and functionality needs cropped up. This versatility would allow Knight to work in an exploratory manner while facilitating speedy development.

The biggest benefit [of Django] was the speed at which we were able to concept and develop out our CMS.

—Eric Schoenborn | Creative Director, Knight Foundation

The Development Process

The actual development process took only four months. Django enabled the front-end developers to write markup following best practices, rather than being constrained by prescribed practices. They were also able to automatically test everything (Django deploys a new version of a site in less than a minute). Scalability meant that the developers could build high-performance features without increasing server load or crashing under traffic. So Knight didn’t have to spend lots of money on hardware, despite their high number of users.

The Results

Since the Django-based KnightFoundation.org launched in June 2011, pageviews have increased 55%, unique visitors to the site have grown by 58%, and the homepage bounce rate improved by 30%. Site stability during traffic spikes is no longer a problem.
